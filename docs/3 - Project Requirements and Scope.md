# Project Requirements and Scope

## 1. Stakeholder map for RoomLight

### High Power, High Influence
- Hotel Chain Management
- Hotel Manager
- IT Support / Technical Staff

### High Power, Low Impact

- Hotel Guests
- Maintenance Team
- Installers

### Low Power, High Impact

- Hotel Marketing Team
- Cleaning Staff

### Low Power, Low Impact

- Competitors
- Media



## 2. What do the Stake Holders want?

### 1. Hotel Owner

**What matters most to them?**
Cost efficiency and that the product actually does what's advertised.

**What keeps them up at night?**
Buying a system that doesn't work or is too expensive.

**Key question**
Does buying RoomLight reduce costs or improve the customer experience in a meaningful way?

**What do we need from them?**
The decision to buy the product, feedback.

### 2. Hotel Management

**What matters most to them?**
Simplicity, not getting in the way of their normal priorities, saving time.

**What keeps them up at night?**
If the product only adds to their workload.

**Key question**
Will this product make the managers job easier or harder?

**What do we need from them?**
To learn to use the product, feedback.

### 3. Hotel Guests

**What matters most to them?**
A pleasant stay at the hotel.

**What keeps them up at night?**
Technical errors, faulty lights or misuse of the product (literally, if the lights don't work or start turning on and off at night.)

**Key question**
Is this system easy to use, will it work?

**What do we need from them?**
Nothing, but the hotel needs feedback from them to form their own opinion on the product.


## 3. Product Requirements

### Core requirements

- The system must allow centralized control of lighting across all rooms.
- It needs to support existing lighting configurations
- It needs to work in multiple rooms and floors at once.
- It has to be more efficient than the previous system the hotel had.

### Usability Requirements

- The interface has to be simple so that the hotel staff can use it

### Technical Requirements

- The system must support remote updates and configutarion changes.
- It has to include diagnostics for troubleshooting.

### Business Requirements

- The system has to demonstrate it's better and more efficient than whatever the hotel was doing before it
- It has to impress the buyer

## 4. Prototype Scope

### What should be included in the prototype

- Ability to create and apply presets and basic configuration for lights
- Simple UI
- Simple remote control functionality

### What doesn't have to be included in the prototype

- Full hardware integration
- Full scalability
- Any other advanced features

### Goals of the prototype
- Demonstrate that we're working on something
- Show off how the final product is supposed to function
